VoteATX.US

Find the best place to vote right now for the May 27, 2014 primary run-off election in Travis County.

This application finds voting places closest to where you are right now.

It knows not only about day-of-the-election voting places, but also early voting places -- both the fixed locations that are available throughout the early voting period as well as mobile locations that move around from day to day.

Depending on your web browser settings, you may be prompted to allow your location to be identified. Once you have been located, it will draw a map showing your position and nearby voting places.

Here are some things you can do with the map:

  • Drag the red "you are here" marker around, to explore a different area.
  • Click a location on the map to find voting places near there.
  • Type an address in the field at the bottom of the screen to map that location.

Elections in the City of Austin are administered by the Travis County Clerk's Election Division.

Voting Places

The "Vote" icons identify voting places for the selected "you are here" location.

A "lit up" icon shows a voting place open right now. A dimmed icon shows a closed voting place.

The election day voting place for the "you are here" location.

This is the place you go to vote on election day.

Note that for some elections, the State of Texas allows "Election Day Vote Centers". For these elections, you can vote at any polling place, not just the one in your assigned neighborhood precinct.

Election day vote centers will be in effect for the this election. That means you may vote at any designated voting place for this election.

The early voting place that is closest to the "you are here" location.

If you are registered to vote in Travis County, then you can vote at any of the early voting places during the early voting period.

A mobile early voting place that is convenient to the "you are here" location.

The mobile early voting places are open only at certain times during the early voting period. If you are registered to vote in Travis County, then you can vote at any mobile early voting place that is open.

The map will display only mobile early voting places that are convenient to your "you are here" location. You won't see any mobile places if you are in a location where the fixed early voting place is the best choice.

Voter Id Required

New Texas voter id requirements are now in effect. You will need to bring a picture id with you when you want to vote. For more information, visit the Keep Calm and Vote On website.
